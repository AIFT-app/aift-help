# Uploading Bank Statements

If your bank account is not connected via open banking, you can import transactions by uploading a PDF bank statement. AI Finance Team reads the statement, extracts each transaction, and links them to the correct bank account automatically.

## Who Can Upload Statements

Any workspace member can upload a bank statement. The upload button is on the **Transactions** page, in the **Statements** tab.

## Supported Formats

Upload PDF bank statements. Scanned PDFs work as long as the text is legible — very low-quality scans may produce incomplete results.

One file per statement. If a statement spans multiple PDFs, upload each file separately.

## How to Upload a Statement

1. Go to **Transactions** in the workspace sidebar.
2. Click the **Statements** tab.
3. Click **Upload statement**.
4. Select your PDF file from your computer.
5. The dialog reads the first page of the statement and tries to identify the bank account automatically (see [Automatic Account Matching](#automatic-account-matching) below).
6. Review the detected account and date range, then click **Start extraction**.

The dialog closes and a progress card appears in the Statements tab. Extraction runs page by page in the background — you can navigate away and come back. The card updates as each page is processed.

When extraction completes, the transactions appear in the **Transactions** tab under the matched bank account.

## Automatic Account Matching

After reading the first page of the statement, AI Finance Team looks up the account number in your workspace to find the matching bank account.

- If a matching account is found, it is shown in the upload dialog before you confirm.
- If no match is found, you will be asked to select the bank account manually from a list of accounts in the workspace.

Matching works with both IBAN format (`HU12 3456 7890 1234 5678 0000 0000`) and domestic account number format (`34567890-12345678`). Spaces and hyphens are ignored during comparison.

If the bank account does not exist yet in your workspace but the account is listed under one of your entities in **Master Data → Entities**, the account is created automatically and linked to that entity.

## Extraction Progress

Each uploaded statement gets a progress card in the Statements tab showing:

- **File name and upload date**
- **Pages processed** out of total pages
- **Transactions found** so far

Once all pages are processed the card shows **Completed** and the transaction count. If a page fails to extract, the card shows **Failed** — click **Re-extract** to try again from scratch.

## What Happens After Extraction

Once extraction completes:

1. All extracted transactions are written to the matched bank account.
2. Categorisation and counterparty resolution run automatically — the same AI pipeline used for open banking transactions.
3. Transactions appear in the **Transactions** tab, ready for review.

Duplicates are detected automatically. If a transaction already exists in the system (imported via open banking or a previous statement upload), it will not be inserted again.

## Reviewing Extracted Transactions

Extracted transactions appear in the **Transactions** tab filtered by the bank account. Review them the same way as any other transaction:

- Click a row to open the detail slide-over on the right.
- Change a category by clicking the category badge and selecting a different one.
- Add a note using the note field in the slide-over.

See [Transactions](/transactions) for the full guide.

## Viewing Statement Details

Click any row in the **Statements** tab to open the statement detail page. This shows:

- The statement header (account number, account holder, period covered)
- Every extracted row — the raw text the AI read from the PDF alongside the structured data it produced
- The status of each page

Use the detail page to investigate extraction errors or verify that the AI read a specific transaction correctly.

## Re-extracting a Statement

If extraction failed or produced incorrect results, open the statement detail page and click **Re-extract**. This resets the statement to pending and starts extraction from the beginning. Existing transactions from this statement are removed before re-extraction.

Re-extraction does not remove transactions that were also imported from another source (for example via open banking).

## Tips

- **Match accounts before uploading.** If you add bank account details under **Master Data → Entities** before uploading, the statement will match automatically and skip the manual selection step.
- **Upload one period at a time.** Overlapping statements from the same account will be deduplicated, but keeping periods clean makes reconciliation easier.
- **Check the Statements tab for status.** If you uploaded a file and see no new transactions, check the Statements tab — the card will show whether extraction is still running or encountered an error.
- **Large statements take longer.** A 20-page statement processes page by page; expect a few minutes for completion.
