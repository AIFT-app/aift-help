# Workspaces

A workspace represents one client engagement. Each workspace belongs to your accounting firm and is shared with one client company. Accountants typically work across many workspaces; client users see only their own.

---

## The Workspaces Dashboard

When you sign in, you land on the **Workspaces** dashboard — your morning overview of where things stand across all clients.

### Status dots

Each workspace tile shows a coloured dot that tells you at a glance whether the workspace needs attention:

| Dot | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| 🟢 Green | Everything is under control — no open issues, fewer than five items in each bucket |
| 🟡 Amber | Something needs checking — a bucket has reached five items, or there is at least one open issue |
| 🔴 Red | Needs attention today — a bucket has fifteen or more items, three or more issues, or work has been sitting unreviewed for more than five days with no recent client uploads |

### The three stat cards

Each tile shows three counts:

- **To review** — bank transactions flagged for human review (AI was uncertain about categorisation)
- **Unmatched** — invoices that have not yet been matched to a bank transaction
- **Issues** — extraction failures and very-low-confidence categorisations

### Sections

Tiles are grouped into two sections:

- **Needs attention** — any workspace with an amber or red dot
- **All caught up** — workspaces where everything is green

The count after each section title updates as you search.

### Searching

Type in the search box at the top right to filter by workspace name. The section structure stays in place as you type — you'll see, for example, "Needs attention (1)" if only one matching workspace is in that bucket. To clear the filter, click **Clear search** or delete the search text.

### Overview banner

Below the page header, a single line summarises your full client book: how many documents need review, how many transactions are unmatched, how many issues are open, and how many workspaces are flagged. This is the one-glance check before you decide which workspace to open first.

## Creating a New Workspace

Only org **owners** and **admins** can create workspaces. Regular accountants work inside workspaces they have been added to — they cannot create new ones. If you need workspace creation access, ask your firm's owner or admin to promote you from the **Org settings** page (see [Roles & Permissions](/roles-and-permissions/)).

### Starting the wizard

On the Workspaces dashboard, click **New workspace** in the top-right action bar. This opens a three-step wizard.

---

### Step 1 — Workspace details

Fill in the basic information for the new client engagement:

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Client name | The client's trading name; becomes the workspace name |
| Country | Drives currency and timezone defaults |
| Base currency | Pre-filled based on country; editable |
| Timezone | Pre-filled based on country; editable |

Selecting a country automatically fills in the most common currency and timezone for that country. You can change either field independently before continuing.

Click **Next** to proceed to Step 2.

---

### Step 2 — Primary entity

A workspace needs at least one entity — the legal company that owns the client's bank accounts and appears on their invoices. AI Finance Team uses the entity to determine invoice direction (income vs. expense), so this step cannot be skipped.

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Entity name | Legal company name — required |
| Tax ID | Strongly recommended; can be added later in master data |
| Country | Defaults to the workspace country |

Additional entities (for multi-company clients) can be added later from the workspace's master data section.

Click **Next** to proceed to Step 3, or **Back** to return to Step 1 without losing your entries.

---

### Step 3 — Invite client (optional)

This step prompts you to invite the client owner so they can connect bank accounts and upload invoices. It is optional — you can skip it and send the invitation later from workspace settings.

If you choose to invite now:

1. Enter the client's email address.
2. Click **Send invitation**.
3. The wizard displays a **copyable invite link** — copy it and share it with your client directly (email, chat, etc.).

> Automated invitation emails are not yet delivered by the platform. You share the link manually.

The invitation is assigned the **client owner** role. The client owner can then invite their colleagues as client members from within the workspace.

Click **Skip** to finish without inviting, or complete the invite step to get the link.

---

### After the wizard

Once the wizard completes, you are redirected to the new workspace's overview page. The workspace appears immediately in your Workspaces dashboard. You are automatically set as an **accountant admin** of the new workspace.

If you did not provide a tax ID for the primary entity, the workspace overview shows a prompt to add it from master data. You can dismiss this prompt and return to it later.

## Joining an Existing Workspace

Org owners and admins can see **all workspaces** belonging to their firm on the dashboard, including workspaces they have not personally joined. These non-member workspaces show a **Join** button.

Clicking **Join** adds you to the workspace immediately as an **accountant admin** and takes you directly to that workspace.

Regular accountants (non-admin org members) do not see the Join button and cannot self-add to workspaces. They must be invited by an accountant admin already in the workspace.

---

## Inviting Team Members

Once a workspace exists, an **accountant admin** can invite additional members from workspace **Settings → Team**:

- Other accountants from your firm (as accountant or accountant admin)
- Client users at the client company (as client owner or client member)

See [Roles & Permissions](/roles-and-permissions/) for the full breakdown of what each role can see and do.

---

## Org Settings

The **Org settings** link in the top-right of the dashboard is visible to org owners and admins. It takes you to your firm's organisation settings, where you can manage team members and their roles across the whole firm.

---

## What's Not Yet Built

- **Bank connection management UI** — bank account data exists but the interface for clients to connect, disconnect, or manage their bank link is not yet available.
- **Removing a workspace member** — only pending invitations can currently be revoked. Removing someone who has already accepted requires a support request.
- **Workspace settings** — the settings page is a stub; configuration like auto-categorisation and missing document alerts are set at workspace creation but cannot yet be changed via the UI.
- **Per-workspace closing cadence** — the closing period shown in each tile footer is currently the current calendar month. Support for quarterly VAT cycles and other reporting cadences is planned.
